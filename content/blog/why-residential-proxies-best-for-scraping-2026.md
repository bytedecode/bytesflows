## Why Residential Proxies Are Best for Scraping
**Residential proxies** use IP addresses assigned by ISPs to real homes and devices. To target sites, your traffic appears to come from normal users, not datacenters. That leads to **fewer blocks**, higher success rates, and better access to strict sites (e.g. [Cloudflare](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/bypass-cloudflare-web-scraping), e‑commerce, SERP). For scraping, they’re usually the best choice when you need scale and reliability. Read [Residential Proxies to Improve Scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies-improve-scraping) and try [Residential Proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies) for production.
## Residential vs Datacenter for Scraping
- **Datacenter** — Cheap and fast, but IPs are known to be hosting/cloud. Many sites rate-limit or block them. See [Datacenter vs Residential Proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/datacenter-vs-residential-proxies).
- **Residential** — Higher cost, but traffic looks like real users. [Best proxies for web scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) lists often favor residential for serious scraping. Use [Proxy Checker](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-checker) to verify IP type and location.
For [rotating proxies for web scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/rotating-proxies-web-scraping) and [proxy rotation strategies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies), residential pools integrate well with rotation (per-request or per-session). See [How Proxy Rotation Works](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies) and [Proxy Rotator](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotator).
## Benefits for Scraping
- **Lower block rates** — Sites treat residential IPs like normal visitors. [Avoiding IP bans](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/avoid-ip-bans-web-scraping) is easier with [residential proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies).
- **Geo-targeting** — Choose country or region for [geo-targeted scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping). Useful for local prices, SERP, or compliance.
- **Access to strict sites** — [Cloudflare](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/bypass-cloudflare-web-scraping), [DataDome](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/handling-captchas-in-scraping), and e‑commerce often allow more residential traffic. [Web scraping without getting blocked](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scrape-websites-without-getting-blocked) relies on this.
- **Scale** — Large [proxy pools](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) and [proxy management](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies) let you distribute load. [How many proxies you need](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies) depends on concurrency and targets.
For architecture, see [Web Scraping Proxy Architecture](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) and [Building Proxy Infrastructure](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies). For Python and Playwright, [Using Proxies with Python](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/python-scraping-proxy) and [Using Proxies with Playwright](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/playwright-web-scraping-tutorial).
## When to Use Residential Proxies
Use [residential proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies) when:
- You scrape at **high volume** or many pages per day. [Rotating proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/rotating-proxies-web-scraping) and [proxy rotation](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies) reduce per-IP load.
- Targets use **anti-bot** ([Cloudflare](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/cloudflare-scraping), fingerprinting). [Bypass Cloudflare](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/bypass-cloudflare-web-scraping) and [browser fingerprinting](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/browser-fingerprinting-explained) are easier with residential IPs.
- You need **geo-specific** data (local SERP, prices). [Best Proxies for Web Scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) and [Geo-Targeted Scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) cover this.
- You run **browser automation** ([Playwright](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/playwright-web-scraping-tutorial), [headless](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/headless-browser-scraping-guide)) and want to avoid detection. [Avoid detection in Playwright](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scrape-websites-without-getting-blocked) and [best practices](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/avoid-ip-bans-web-scraping) apply.
[Common proxy mistakes](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/avoid-ip-bans-web-scraping) include using only datacenter IPs for strict sites or under-rotating. Use [Proxy Checker](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-checker) and [Scraping Test](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scraping-test) to validate. For product options, see [Proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/proxies) and [Residential Proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies).
## How Residential Proxies Are Sourced
Residential proxy networks route your traffic through IPs that belong to real ISP subscribers (with consent, via apps or partnerships). The target site sees a normal home or mobile IP, not a datacenter range. That’s why [how websites detect scrapers](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/how-websites-detect-scrapers) often focuses on ASN and IP reputation: datacenter blocks are well known, residential are not. When you use [rotating residential proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies), the provider’s gateway assigns a new IP from the pool per request or per session. See [how proxy rotation works](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies) and [proxy rotation strategies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies). [Proxy Rotator](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotator) lets you test rotation behaviour. [Best proxies for web scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) and [residential proxies improve scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies-improve-scraping) explain why this improves success rates.
## Use Cases Where Residential Wins
- **E‑commerce and marketplaces** — Product and price scraping often hit [Cloudflare](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/bypass-cloudflare-web-scraping) or similar. [Residential proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies) and [using proxies with Playwright](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/using-proxies-playwright) are the norm. [Scraping e‑commerce](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scraping-ecommerce-websites) and [scraping Amazon](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scraping-amazon-product-data) depend on it.
- **SERP and search** — [Scraping Google](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/how-to-scrape-google) and [scraping SERP](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scraping-serp-data) are heavily rate-limited from datacenter IPs. [Geo-targeted scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) with residential IPs by country is common.
- **Social and job boards** — [Scraping social media](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scraping-social-media-data) and [scraping job listings](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/scraping-job-listings) often require [residential proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies) and [avoiding IP bans](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/avoid-ip-bans-web-scraping).
- **Anti-bot and CAPTCHA** — [Bypass Cloudflare](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/bypass-cloudflare-web-scraping), [handling CAPTCHAs](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/handling-captchas-in-scraping), and [browser fingerprinting](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/browser-fingerprinting-explained) are easier with residential IPs and a real browser.
## Cost vs Benefit
Residential proxies cost more per GB or per IP than datacenter. The benefit is **fewer blocks**, less engineering time on workarounds, and access to sites that would otherwise be impractical. For small or one-off jobs, [datacenter vs residential](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/datacenter-vs-residential-proxies) might still allow datacenter; for production and strict targets, [best proxies for web scraping](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/best-proxies-for-web-scraping) and [residential proxies](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/residential-proxies) are the default. [Proxy management for large scrapers](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies) and [how many proxies you need](https://bytesflows.com/en/blog/proxy-rotation-strategies) help size your pool.
